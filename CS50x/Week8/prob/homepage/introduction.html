The Origin of Billiards

Historical Background

The origin of billiards dates back to the 15th century. Its initial form was played on the ground before evolving into a table game. The game first gained popularity in Europe, particularly in England.

Initially, billiards did not resemble the modern game of snooker played on a pool table. Instead, it was an indoor sport involving cues and balls. Over time, the rules of billiards evolved, leading to the familiar form we know today.

Evolution and Spread

The definitive form of billiards began to take shape in the late 19th century and became immensely popular in England and the United States. This period marked the transition of billiards from a noble entertainment to a widely enjoyed sport.

With increasing international exchanges, billiards spread worldwide, adapting to unique cultural influences in different regions. In the 20th century, billiards evolved into an international competitive sport, attracting professional players and a broad audience.
